## Overview

Hydra (*Hydra vulgaris*) is a unique cnidarian model organism renowned for its extraordinary regenerative capabilities and developmental plasticity. As one of the simplest animals with a nervous system, Hydra serves as an important model for studying fundamental biological processes including regeneration, stem cell biology, and the evolution of multicellularity. This portal provides genomic resources for investigating the molecular mechanisms underlying head regeneration and tissue patterning in this remarkable organism.

## Research Areas Covered

- **Regenerative Biology**: Head regeneration, tissue patterning, wound healing
- **Developmental Biology**: Body axis formation, cell fate determination
- **Comparative Genomics**: Gene expression regulation during regeneration
